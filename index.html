My education in Sri Lanka and now at Hochschule Rhein-Waal has built a strong foundation in biomaterials science. I combine lab techniques in cell biology and microbiology with an understanding of metallic, polymeric, ceramic, and composite materials, as well as REACH and EU regulatory frameworks.

I have hands-on experience in mammalian cell culture, transfection, fluorescent labeling, bacterial growth and MIC determination, and silver nanoparticle coatings for antimicrobial surfaces. I use MATLAB and Ansys for numerical modeling and finite element analysis, and I am focused on biocompatible and biodegradable materials for biomedical applications.

I value teamwork, precision, and adaptability—whether in the lab, at Kaufland, or during my optical assistant role in Sri Lanka. I am motivated by research that connects materials science with real-world impact in healthcare and industry.
